## Quick Contrast Anchors

### Perception & Intelligence
- **Perspicacious** minds see; **heedful** minds notice.
- **Sagacious** is wisdom in action; **erudite** is book learning.
- **Fatuous** is smug foolishness; **vapid** is mere emptiness.
- **Myopic** is too close to see; **blinkered** is shielded from the sides.

### Intellectual Failure Modes
- **Tendentious** serves an agenda; **specious** looks true but isn't.
- **Sophistic** is deliberately misleading; **captious** is needlessly critical.
- **Pedantic** obsesses over rules; **doctrinaire** clings to doctrine.
- **Parochial** is narrow in scope; **insular** is isolated from outside views.

### Development & Change
- **Inchoate** ideas grow; **dilapidated** things decay.
- **Nascent** suggests promise; **moribund** suggests dying.
- **Protean** things change form; **immutable** things never change.

### Effort & Quality
- **Alacritous** action contrasts with **perfunctory** effort.
- **Meticulous** work is careful; **slipshod** work is careless.
- **Superlative** quality is the best; **execrable** is the worst.
- **Scrupulous** attends to ethics; **conscientious** attends to duty.

### Emotional Regulation
- **Composed** is calm in manner; **collected** is calm in thought.
- **Measured** is proportionate; **dispassionate** is analytically removed.
- **Stoic** endures without complaint; **phlegmatic** feels without reacting.
- **Irascible** explodes; **choleric** simmers; **peevish** complains.

### Courage & Cowardice
- **Intrepid** is fearless; **pusillanimous** is contemptibly timid; **craven** is despicably cowardly.
- **Valorous** acts despite danger; **recreant** betrays duty; **dastardly** is both cowardly and cruel.

### Moral Dissolution
- **Dissolute** is morally loose; **profligate** is recklessly wasteful; **debauched** is corrupted by excess.
- **Reprobate** is irredeemably depraved; **licentious** is unrestrained; **turpitude** is the state itself.

### Shame & Disgrace
- **Ignominious** describes the act; **ignominy** is the resulting state.
- **Opprobrium** is public condemnation; **obloquy** is verbal abuse; **contumely** is insulting treatment.
- **Infamy** is fame inverted — known for wickedness rather than virtue.

### Tone & Judgment
- **Macabre** describes tone; **odious** describes moral judgment.
- **Sardonic** is bitter mockery; **jovial** is cheerful friendliness.
- **Magnanimous** giving is noble; **munificent** giving is lavish.

### Social Virtue & Failure
- **Tactful** knows what to say; **diplomatic** knows how; **discreet** knows when not to.
- **Churlish** is rude by nature; **boorish** is rude by ignorance.
- **Supercilious** looks down; **haughty** holds high; **imperious** demands.
- **Forbearance** restrains reaction; **circumspection** restrains action.

### Speech & Expression
- **Laconic** speech is brief; **taciturn** people are quiet; **reticent** people hold back.
- **Eloquent** speech is moving; **bombastic** speech is hollow.
- **Pithy** statements are concise; **prolix** ones are tedious.
- **Glib** is fluent but shallow; **facund** is genuinely fluent.

### Light & Sound
- **Luminous** things glow; **resplendent** things dazzle; **lambent** things shimmer softly.
- **Mellifluous** sounds are sweet; **cacophonous** sounds are harsh; **strident** sounds are sharp.

### Truth & Deception
- **Mendacious** people lie; **disingenuous** people mislead.
- **Veracious** speech is truthful; **specious** arguments seem true but aren't.
- **Probity** is proven integrity; **rectitude** is moral correctness.

### Connection & Conflict
- **Amity** is friendship; **enmity** is hostility.
- **Accord** is harmony; **discord** is conflict.
- **Rancor** is deep bitterness; **acrimony** is sharp bitterness.

### Staining & Damaging
- **Besmirch** and **sully** suggest moral staining; **tarnish** suggests loss of luster.
- **Defile** violates sanctity; **pollute** spreads contamination; **taint** introduces corruption.
- **Demean** lowers dignity; **degrade** strips it; **abase** is reflexive submission.
- **Slander** is spoken; **libel** is written; **defame** covers both.

### Orthodoxy & Dissent
- **Iconoclast** attacks beliefs; **heretic** holds wrong ones; **apostate** abandons them.
- **Orthodox** conforms to doctrine; **heterodox** departs from it; **dogmatic** insists on it.
- **Maverick** refuses to conform; **contrarian** actively opposes; **gadfly** persistently provokes.
- **Dissident** opposes policy; **renegade** deserts the cause; **subversive** undermines from within.

### Abundance & Scarcity
- **Plethora** is excess; **paucity** is insufficiency.
- **Copious** is plentiful; **meager** is scanty.
- **Surfeit** is too much; **dearth** is too little.

### Pretension & Identity
- **Poseur** fakes it consciously; **manqué** failed to become it.
- **Parvenu** has the money but not the manners; **arriviste** has the ambition but not the scruples.
- **Affected** is the manner; **factitious** is the substance; **simulacrum** is the whole thing.

### Urgency & Threat
- **Exigent** demands action now; **inexorable** cannot be stopped.
- **Minatory** threatens; **admonitory** warns; **premonitory** foreshadows.
- **Baleful** implies malevolent influence; **baneful** implies destructive effect.
- **Parlous** is literary peril; **dire** is plain-spoken crisis.

### Stubbornness & Persuasion
- **Obdurate** is hard-hearted; **intransigent** is uncompromising; **recalcitrant** kicks back.
- **Cajole** is warm coaxing; **wheedle** is cloying flattery; **inveigle** is deceptive charm.
- **Adjure** is solemn; **importune** is persistent; **browbeat** is aggressive.
- **Contumacious** is the legal register; **mulish** is the barnyard register.

### Reputation & Moral Judgment
- **Renown** is earned by achievement; **notoriety** is earned by scandal.
- **Prestige** implies admiration; **cachet** implies exclusivity.
- **Venial** (pardonable) vs. **venal** (corrupt) — opposite in gravity.
- **Faustian** implies a knowing sacrifice; **Pyrrhic** implies an unknowing cost.
- **Manichean** reduces to binary; **Solomonic** navigates complexity.
